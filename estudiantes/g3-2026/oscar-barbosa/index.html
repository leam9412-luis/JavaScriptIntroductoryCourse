About Me

It started in childhood when I got a Super Nintendo — I was immediately fascinated by the question: how do they make this? I tried to break into game development back then, but the internet and self-learning resources weren't widely accessible, so I never found the right guidance. I never stopped playing though, always thinking about what was happening under the hood.

Years later, life took me into medicine. I graduated as a General Practitioner and spent most of my career practicing medicine. But the passion never left. As the internet evolved and self-teaching became accessible, I started programming as a hobby — then a passion — and now it's my life's calling.

My personal mission: to build simulation experiences that integrate my medical expertise and game development skills, designed for physician training and clinical qualification — aligned with institutional standards and international medical guidelines.
